1. Scope; Entity; Roles

This Privacy Policy describes how AFK GAMES STUDIO (“Studio,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) processes information in connection with the mobile application XO Arcade: Infinity Edition (the “App”).

The App is designed as an offline-first experience. We do not require account registration, and we do not directly request or collect information such as legal names, physical addresses, or account credentials.

However, the App uses third-party SDKs (e.g., Google AdMob and Google Play Services) that may process identifiers and technical information (which may be considered “personal data” or “personal information” under certain laws) as described below.

2. Information Processed on Your Device (Offline-First)

2.1 Local Game Data

The App stores certain information locally on your device to provide core functionality, including:

  • Game State: progress, settings, and gameplay statistics.
  • ZERO Protocol Data: gameplay telemetry used to adjust CPU difficulty and gameplay behavior locally. This data is non-biometric and is processed on-device.

2.2 User Controls (Local Deletion)

You can delete local game data using in-game functions such as “Wipe Memory” and/or “Reset Stats”. Uninstalling the App typically removes locally stored data, subject to your device and operating system backup settings.

3. Information Processed by Third Parties (Automated Collection)

The App integrates third-party SDKs for advertising and platform functionality. These third parties may automatically collect or receive certain information, which may include:

  • Advertising/Device Identifiers: Google Advertising ID (or equivalent identifiers), device identifiers, and app instance identifiers.
  • Network/Location Signals: IP address and approximate location inferred from IP.
  • Device/Technical Data: device model, hardware/OS version, language, time zone, app version, diagnostics, and performance data.
  • Advertising Data: ad impressions, clicks, ad interaction events, and anti-fraud signals.

We do not operate our own analytics or advertising servers for these functions; instead, third parties process this information under their own terms and policies and, in some cases, as independent controllers.

4. How Information Is Used

We use (or enable third parties to use) information to:

  • Provide and maintain App functionality (e.g., saving local progress and settings).
  • Improve gameplay experience (e.g., adjusting CPU difficulty using on-device ZERO Protocol data).
  • Display, measure, and optimize ads; prevent fraud and abuse; enforce policy compliance.
  • Maintain security and stability (e.g., troubleshooting, crash/performance diagnostics).
  • Comply with legal obligations.

5. Legal Bases (EEA/UK)

Where GDPR/UK GDPR applies:

  • Consent: We rely on your consent for personalized advertising where required. Consent choices are managed through the Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) or equivalent in-app consent flow.
  • Legitimate Interests: We and/or our partners may process limited technical and security information to provide a secure and stable App, prevent fraud, and measure performance, subject to your rights and applicable law.
  • Contract Necessity: Local processing needed to provide the core offline App experience (e.g., saving progress locally).

7. Your Choices; Privacy Rights

7.1 Consent & Ads Preferences (UMP)

Where available, you can review or change ad consent choices using the in-app “Privacy Options” (or equivalent) which surfaces UMP choices.

7.2 Device Controls

Depending on your device and region, you may be able to reset or delete your Advertising ID and limit ad personalization through device settings (for example, by searching “Ads” or “Advertising” in system settings).

7.3 California (CCPA/CPRA)

We do not “sell” personal information for money. Depending on how “share” is defined under applicable law, certain advertising-related disclosures may be considered “sharing” for cross-context behavioral advertising. Where required, we provide an option to opt out of such “sale” or “sharing” via in-app “Privacy Options” or applicable device settings. California residents may also have rights to access, delete, or correct certain personal information, subject to verification and legal exceptions.

7.4 EEA/UK and Other Regions

Subject to applicable law, you may have rights to access, delete, or object to certain processing. To submit a request, contact us as described in Section 13. Please note that we may be unable to access or delete information held by third parties directly; in those cases, you must use the controls provided by the third party or your device settings.

8. Data Retention

  • Third-party data retention is governed by the third parties’ own policies and settings.

9. Children’s Privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided information through the App, contact us and we will take appropriate steps consistent with applicable law.

10. Security

We use reasonable administrative and technical measures designed to protect information. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

11. International Transfers

If you use the App outside the United States, information processed by third-party partners may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own, including the United States, subject to the partners’ safeguards and policies and applicable law.

12. Data Deletion (How to Delete Data)

12.1 Local (On-Device) Deletion

Because the App is offline-first, most App data is stored locally on your device. You can delete local game data by:

  • Using in-app controls such as “Wipe Memory” and/or “Reset Stats”; and/or
  • Uninstalling the App (subject to your device/OS backup settings).

12.2 Third-Party Data

Some information may be processed by third-party SDKs (e.g., Google AdMob / Google Play Services). The Studio may not be able to directly access, delete, or control data held by third parties. Where available, use:

  • The App’s “Privacy Options” (Google UMP) to manage advertising consent/choices; and/or
  • Your device settings (e.g., reset/delete Advertising ID or limit ad personalization); and/or
  • The third party’s own controls and policies.

12.3 Requests

If you have a privacy or deletion request that applies to Studio-controlled processing, contact us using the details in Section 13.
